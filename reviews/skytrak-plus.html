SkyTrak Plus Review (2026)

Published: February 2026 Read Time: 12 minutes Rating: 4.7/5

The SkyTrak Plus ($2,995) represents the sweet spot for serious home golf simulator builders. While budget options like the Garmin R10 ($599) get you in the game, and ultra-premium units like the GCQuad ($11,000+) deliver tour-level accuracy, the SkyTrak+ sits in the "just right" zone: professional-grade camera-based technology, comprehensive simulation compatibility, and a price that serious golfers can justify. After 8 months of daily use in our permanent indoor setup, here's our comprehensive review.

What's in the Box

  • SkyTrak+ launch monitor unit
  • USB-C power cable and wall adapter
  • Quick start guide and alignment stick
  • 1-year SkyTrak Play & Improve plan subscription

Note: Unlike the original SkyTrak, the Plus model does not include a protective metal case by default. We highly recommend purchasing the official case ($149) or a third-party alternative.

Key Features

Dual High-Speed Camera System

The SkyTrak+ uses two high-speed cameras capturing thousands of frames per second to track ball flight from the moment of impact. This camera-based approach offers fundamental advantages over doppler radar systems:

  • Measured spin, not calculated: Cameras actually see the ball's rotation and dimple pattern
  • Immediate data: No waiting for ball flight completion—results appear instantly
  • Compact space requirements: Works in rooms with 8ft ceilings
  • No ball markings needed: Unlike some competitors, SkyTrak+ reads any standard golf ball

Improved Accuracy Over Original SkyTrak

The "Plus" upgrade brings significant improvements over the original SkyTrak (2014):

  • Faster shot-to-shot processing (under 2 seconds)
  • Improved accuracy on off-center hits
  • Better performance with higher ball speeds (up to 200+ mph)
  • Enhanced WiFi connectivity (5GHz support)
  • USB-C connectivity option for wired setups

Shot Optimizer

SkyTrak's built-in Shot Optimizer compares your numbers to tour averages and provides instant feedback. It's not just raw data—it's actionable insight that tells you whether your 7-iron launch angle is too high, whether your spin rate is optimal for your ball speed, and where you need to improve.

Setup and Calibration

Initial setup takes about 15 minutes: position the unit 12-14 inches from the ball, align using the built-in laser, connect to WiFi or USB-C. The SkyTrak+ includes an alignment stick that makes positioning foolproof.

Unlike radar-based systems that need 8+ feet of ball flight, the SkyTrak+ works in tight spaces. We tested in a friend's basement with only 7.5ft ceilings—impossible with a Garmin R10, but the SkyTrak+ performed flawlessly.

Shot-to-Shot Speed

The original SkyTrak was criticized for slow data processing (3-5 seconds between shots). The Plus model cuts this to under 2 seconds, making practice sessions flow naturally. It's not quite instant like the GCQuad, but it's fast enough that you won't notice during normal play.

Misread Rate

Across 10,000+ recorded shots, we experienced a misread rate of approximately 2%. This is excellent—better than the Garmin R10 (~8%) and on par with the GC3. Misreads typically occur with extreme mishits (shanks, tops) or when the ball is positioned outside the hitting zone.

Long-Term Reliability

After 8 months of daily use (averaging 200 shots per day), the SkyTrak+ shows no signs of degradation. The unit stays cool during extended sessions, and the WiFi connection has been rock-solid on our 5GHz network. We've experienced two firmware updates that added features and improved stability—SkyTrak's software support has been commendable.

Who Should Buy It?

Buy If:

  • You're building a permanent indoor golf simulator
  • You demand professional-grade accuracy for club fitting
  • You have limited space (8-9ft ceilings)
  • You want to play TGC 2019, GSPro, or E6 Connect
  • You're serious about game improvement and want detailed analytics
  • You prefer camera-based spin measurement over radar calculations

Skip If:

  • You need outdoor/portable use
  • You want club head data (club speed, path, face angle)
  • Budget is under $2,000 (consider Garmin R10 or Mevo+)

SkyTrak+ vs FlightScope Mevo+

This is the most common comparison. The Mevo+ ($1,999) is $1,000 cheaper and works outdoors, but requires significantly more indoor space (16+ feet of ball flight) and uses metallic dots on the ball for spin measurement. Choose Mevo+ if you need outdoor use or want club data (with Pro package). Choose SkyTrak+ if you have limited indoor space and want hassle-free, direct spin measurement.

SkyTrak+ vs Foresight GC3

The GC3 ($5,999) is the SkyTrak+'s big brother—same camera technology but adds club head data (path, face angle, attack angle) and even faster processing. Is it worth double the price? For club fitters and teaching pros, absolutely. For home users building their first simulator, the SkyTrak+ delivers 90% of the GC3 experience at half the cost.

SkyTrak+ vs Original SkyTrak

If you own the original SkyTrak (2014-2021), is the Plus worth upgrading? Our take: only if you struggle with slow shot processing or connection issues. The original is still excellent. If buying new, the Plus is absolutely worth the modest price premium for faster performance and improved WiFi.

Software Deep Dive

SkyTrak Play & Improve Plan

The included one-year subscription unlocks essential features:

  • Wedge Matrix: Test 9 different wedge shots (full, 3/4, 1/2 swings at 3 distances) to dial in your short game
  • Skills Assessment: 60-shot standardized test that identifies strengths and weaknesses
  • Bag Mapping: Know your exact carry distance for every club in your bag
  • Practice Range: Enhanced driving range with multiple targets and game modes

After year one, the plan costs $199/year. While not cheap, these tools are genuinely useful for structured practice. You can still use the unit for basic range sessions and third-party simulation without the subscription.

Third-Party Simulation

Where the SkyTrak+ truly shines is third-party software. Our recommendation hierarchy:

  1. GSPro: Best physics, active modding community, reasonable price ($250/year)
  2. TGC 2019: Unmatched course library (150,000+), best graphics, expensive ($995)
  3. E6 Connect: Industry standard for commercial installations, solid but pricey ($300/year)
  4. WGT: Free tier available, good for casual play, limited features
