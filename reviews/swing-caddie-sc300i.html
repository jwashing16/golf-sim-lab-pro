The Swing Caddie SC300i ($449) fills a specific niche: golfers who want launch monitor data without the hassle of connecting a phone or tablet. With its built-in display and voice output, it's the ultimate grab-and-go practice tool.

Real-World Testing

Range Performance

The SC300i excels at the driving range. Setup takes under 30 seconds: place it 4-5 feet behind the ball, turn it on, select your club, and hit.

Indoor Use

The SC300i works indoors but with limitations. It needs 8ft+ ceilings and provides fewer metrics than outdoor use (no apex height indoors).

Battery Life

Voice Caddie claims 12 hours. We consistently got 10-12 hours of range use. The unit charges via USB-C in about 2 hours.

The Verdict

Rating: 4.0/5

The Swing Caddie SC300i is the best standalone launch monitor. If you refuse to deal with phone apps at the range, this is your unit.

However, for $150 more, the Garmin R10 offers significantly more functionality: 10 metrics, simulation compatibility, and indoor/outdoor use. Most golfers will get more value from the R10.

Buy the SC300i if standalone simplicity is your top priority. Otherwise, stretch your budget for the R10.
