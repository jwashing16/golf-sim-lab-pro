Rapsodo Mobile Launch Monitor Review (2026)

Published: February 2026 Read Time: 8 minutes Rating: 4.2/5

The Rapsodo Mobile Launch Monitor ($299) takes a different approach: instead of building radar hardware, it uses your iPhone's camera combined with a small radar unit to measure ball flight. The result? The cheapest launch monitor that actually works.

What's in the Box

  • Rapsodo MLM unit (small radar pod)
  • Phone mount/stand
  • USB charging cable
  • Carry case
  • Free Rapsodo app

How It Works

Unlike the Garmin R10 or FlightScope Mevo+, the Rapsodo MLM relies heavily on your iPhone's camera:

  1. Radar unit tracks initial ball speed and direction
  2. iPhone camera records ball flight and calculates metrics
  3. App combines both data sources in real-time

The clever part: the video recording lets you see a shot tracer replay of every shot.

Key Features

6 Measured Metrics

  1. Ball speed
  2. Club speed
  3. Smash factor
  4. Launch angle
  5. Launch direction
  6. Carry distance

Shot Tracer Video

GPS Satellite View

The app shows your shots overlaid on a satellite view of your location. You can see exactly where each shot would have landed on an actual course.

Real-World Testing

Outdoor Performance

Tested at a driving range with the iPhone 14 Pro:

Metric Accuracy vs TrackMan Notes
Ball Speed Plus/minus 3 mph Good

Bottom line: Accuracy is "good enough" for practice, but not precise enough for serious club fitting.

Indoor Performance

The Rapsodo MLM does not work indoors. The camera needs to see the full ball flight, which requires 15+ feet of space and good lighting.

iPhone Requirements

  • Works with: iPhone 8 and newer
  • Does NOT work with: Android phones, iPads
  • Best results: iPhone 12 Pro or newer (better cameras)

Pros and Cons

Pros

  • Best value under $300 - nothing else comes close
  • Shot tracer videos - fun and useful for swing analysis
  • No subscription required - one-time purchase
  • Super portable - fits in your golf bag pocket
  • GPS view - see where shots land on actual courses
  • Easy setup - under 1 minute

Cons

  • iPhone only - no Android support
  • No indoor use - outdoor only
  • Less accurate - not as precise as R10 or Mevo+
  • No simulation - can't connect to E6 Connect or TGC 2019
  • Limited metrics - no spin rate, club path, or face angle
  • Phone battery drain - uses significant battery during sessions

Who Should Buy It?

Buy If:

  • You have an iPhone (8 or newer)
  • You primarily practice outdoors
  • Budget is tight (under $300)
  • You want shot tracer videos
  • You don't need simulation software
  • You want something extremely portable

Skip If:

  • You have an Android phone
  • You need indoor functionality
  • You want to play simulated rounds
  • You need club path/face angle data
  • You have a bigger budget (get the R10)

The Verdict

Rating: 4.2/5

The Rapsodo MLM is the best ultra-budget launch monitor. It delivers 70% of the functionality of units that cost 2-3x more.

For recreational golfers who want basic metrics and fun shot videos, it's perfect. The shot tracer feature alone justifies the price for many users.

However, if you're serious about improving your game or want to build a home simulator, save up for the Garmin R10. The indoor functionality and simulation compatibility are worth the extra $300.

Recommended setup:

  • iPhone 12 Pro or newer
  • Outdoor driving range or backyard with 20+ feet of space
  • Good lighting (works best on sunny days)
  • Portable battery pack (for long sessions)
